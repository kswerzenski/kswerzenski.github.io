About Me

If there is one thing that working in the field of conservation biology has taught me, it's this: scientists are great at collecting data. But actually putting that data to use? That's the real challenge. After seeing this trend of valuable data about the world around us sitting unused in spreadsheets and drawers, I knew there had to be easier ways to make this data work for us. Enter data science.

I made it my new mission to bridge this gap between conservation and data science, seeking to understand the tools and technology available to transform complex biological data into actionable insights.
